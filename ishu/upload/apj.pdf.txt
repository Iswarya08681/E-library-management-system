A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931-2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman, known as the "Missile Man of India," who played a crucial role in India's space and missile programs (SLV-III, Agni, Prithvi), the 1998 nuclear tests, and served as India's 11th President (2002-2007), becoming the "People's President" for inspiring youth, before passing away during a lecture in 2015. Born in Rameswaram, he rose from humble beginnings to become a revered national figure, earning India's highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna. 
Early Life & Education
Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, to a boat owner.
Struggles: Despite family poverty, he excelled in studies, selling newspapers to help his family.
Education: Graduated in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). 
Scientific Career (DRDO & ISRO)
DRDO (1960s): Started as a scientist, working on small hovercraft.
ISRO (1969): Joined ISRO, becoming Project Director for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully launched the Rohini satellite in 1980, making India a space power.
Missile Development: Instrumental in developing ballistic missiles like Agni and Prithvi. 
Political Career & Presidency
Pokhran-II (1998): Played a key role in India's second nuclear test.
President (2002-2007): Elected as the 11th President, focusing on science, technology, and inspiring youth, earning the "People's President" title. 
Legacy & Death
Inspiration: Known for his simplicity, vision, and motivational speeches, especially to students.
Death: Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.
Awards: Recipient of the Bharat Ratna and numerous other honors
